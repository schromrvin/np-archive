Back in time

From a single campus and a bold vision in 1963, a journey began that would shape generations of learners and transform a community into a national institution.

Chapter 1

Founding Years

1963
On 25 May 1963, Ngee Ann College was officially inaugurated at the Teochew Building, opening its door to its first cohort of students under the leadership of Singapore’s first female college principal, Professor Liu Yin Soon.
The passing of the Ngee Ann College Act marked the College’s transition from a private institution to a publicly governed one under an independent Council.

1968
– Move to Clementi
A New Home : Clementi
The move to the Clementi campus from Teochew Building marked the first time Ngee Ann students studied on the same grounds that today’s students walk, linking six decades of campus life to the same landscape.
A New Name: Ngee Ann Technical College
To reflect its expanded role and focus on technical education, the College Council formally announced the change of name on 22 August 1968 to Ngee Ann Technical College (NATC).
The First Council at Clementi
This Council shaped policies that would eventually lead to Ngee Ann’s recognition as a polytechnic in 1982.
